Deck Construction

A deck only consists of four cards Each player starts with all four cards in their hand Each player plays with their hand revealed; there is no hidden information Players draw cards but do not lose the game for being unable to draw a card Random effects end in the result most beneficial to the opponent of the owner of the card generating the effect (Hymn to Tourach will resolve the same as Mind Rot would) This will be a singleton format, meaning you can only use one copy of each card in your deck (with the exception of basic lands, Relentless Rats, and Shadowborn Apostle (good luck with that)) You may use any Magic card that is not on the ban list (an evolving list—see above) Remember, this is a theoretical format, so you don't actually have to own the cards nor do you actually physically play them

Running the tournament:

Submission will open and close at designated times. Once submissions are closed, all decks will play a match against all other decks. That means that if 16 decks are submitted, they will perform 15 matches, once against each other deck. For each match, there are two games: one on the play and one on the draw. Match results are WIN, LOSS, and DRAW. A match win is WW, WD, or DW. A match loss is LL, LD, or DL. A match draw is WL, LW, or DD. Match wins are worth three points. Match draws are worth one point. Match losses are worth zero. That means that in a 16 deck event, the maximum number of points one can receive is 45, three points for each of the 15 matches. Decks are played out by the moderators. Results will be kept in a spreadsheet, and, alongside decklists, will be posted upon completion before the next round's submissions open.

Player Eligibility

All players must:
  • Be actively enrolled in a high school accredited in the United States
  • Have a North America League of Legends account in good standing and eligible for ranked games. Players may not change their Summoner name without permission during the Event
  • Eligible to participate in school sanctioned club or competitive sports, as defined by their respective high school.
Multiple teams from the same school ARE able to participate in the Event.

Team Management and Roster Rules

Each team consists of (at minimum) 1 Faculty/Staff Contact and 5 active players. Each team is allowed to have up to 2 substitutes which must be registered at the same time as the active roster.

Event Structure:

Seeding

Seeding will be based on the average MMR of the teams participating. Average MMR of each player will be taken from their op.gg profile pages submitted during event registration.

Rounds

  • Matches will be scheduled before the end of the round. Contact information for the Team Captains will be exchanged before the start of the round. Team Captains will agree on a date to play before the end of the round and send that information to the Tournament Admin via Discord.
  • If both Team Captains are unable to find a date that week to play their match….
  • If one team is unresponsive or complete unwilling to play the match at an appropriate time, the opposing team may be declared the winner.
  • It is the responsibility of the Team Captain to schedule and report matches. Communication is of the upmost importance to guarantee an appropriate match time can be found in the event of schedule conflicts. A full week is given for each round of matches.
  • If both teams are unresponsive, or unwilling/unable to find a time where the match for that week can be played, both teams will be removed from both the winners bracket and the losers bracket ultimately disqualifying them from the tournament. For the following round, a bye will occur due to the lack of team participation.

Top 4

At the end of the qualifying bracket, the top 4 teams from the winners bracket and the top 2 teams from the losers bracket will compete at 901 W. New York St. Indianapolis, IN 46202 on April 15th, 2017. All participants are invited to spectate at the event and participate in side-activities. Details of the portion of the Event on April 15th will be shared with all participants at least 30 days prior.

Bracket Details

The bracket will be a Double Elimination Best-of-3 up until there are 4 teams remaining in the winners bracket and 2 teams remaining from the losers bracket.

Match Process:

Referees:

The Tournament Admin will act as the referee for the Event. The tournament admin will be available in the official Discord channel available here - Discord All matches must be reported to the Tournament Admin.

Late Teams

Teams that are not ready to play at the agreed-upon start time are subject to lateness penalties. A team that is ready has all 5 players in the game lobby. A team will automatically forfeit if they are more than 20 minutes late. Intentionally delaying the lobby or game start time will subject the team to further penalties decided upon by the Tournament Admin. The team that is ready (5 players in lobby) must submit a time-stamped screenshot showing the opposing team was not ready 20+ minutes after the agreed-upon start time.

Anti-Gambling Policy

There will absolutely be no gambling during the Event.

Pausing

Teams/players may pause the game for any reason. After a pause, the pausing team must share the reason for the pause with the opposing team and give an estimated time to un-pause. Pauses will not last longer than 10 minutes over the course of a game or 20 minutes over the course of a match.

Side Selection

The higher-ranked seed will have side selection of odd-numbered games (Games 1 and 3), while the lower seed will have side selection for even-numbered games (Game 2). If no decision is submitted, it will default to blue side.

Lobby Creation and Champion Select

The higher-ranked seed will be responsible for creating the lobby. All lobbies will be created as a custom lobby with the name being the school acronyms. For example, for Carmel High School vs Warren Central High School, the name of the custom lobby will be "CHS vs WCHS". Team Size will be set to 5. Passwords are optional. The Game mode will be Tournament Draft and Spectators will be set to Lobby Only. The team that makes the lobby is responsible for inviting the opposing team. In the event of an error in selecting the desired champion pick/ban, the team in error must immediately notify the opposing team as well as the TA before the other team has locked in their next selection. It is recommended to take a time-stamped screenshot of the message being sent before the opposing team locks in their next selection.

Player Conduct

Nor unfair play. No collusion. No hacking. No exploiting. No Ringing. No scripting. No un-approved devices to assist with gameplay. No intentional disconnection. No hate speech. No disruptive behavior or insults. No abusive behavior. No unprofessional behavior. No discrimination. No criminal activity. No bribery. No match-fixing. Any of these actions may cause the team to lose their match or be disqualified from the tournament. If you encounter any of these actions, report it to the Tournament Admin and a decision will be made by Gamers Hall and the Tournament Admin.

Finality of Decisions

All decisions regarding the interpretation of these rules, player eligibility, scheduling, and penalties for misconduct lie solely with Gamers Hall. The decisions are final. Gamers Hall decisions with respect to these rules cannot be appealed and shall not give rise to any claim for monetary damages or other legal or equitable remedy.
